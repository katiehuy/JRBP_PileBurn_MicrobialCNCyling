This repository contains the raw data accompanying the manuscript "Microbially-Mediated Soil Carbon and Nitrogen Cycling After Prescribed Fire."
Post-fire recovery of soil carbon (C) and nitrogen (N) is strongly governed by microbial communities, yet few studies couple microbial dynamics with geochemical transformations at high temporal resolution. This study investigates interactions between microbial community assembly and ash-derived C and N transformation following prescribed pile burns in California chaparral and coast live oak woodland soils.
Data were collected over a one-year field campaign, integrating:

Soil geochemistry — soluble organic matter pools, inorganic N speciation, and pH
In-field CO₂ flux measurements
16S rRNA amplicon sequencing (bacterial and archaeal community composition)
